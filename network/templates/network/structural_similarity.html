Structural equivalence (Lorrain & White 1971) — the cosine between each pair of channels' tie profiles: their weighted out-citations and in-citations to and from every other channel. Two channels score 1.0 when they cite, and are cited by, the same channels with the same relative intensity — i.e. they occupy interchangeable positions in the network, regardless of whether they cite each other.

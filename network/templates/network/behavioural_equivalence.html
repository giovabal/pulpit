Behavioural equivalence — the cosine between each pair of channels' behavioural-measure profiles: amplification, content originality, diffusion lag, and audience/activity volume (followers, message count). The volume features are log-scaled (so a few very large channels don't dominate), then measures are min-max normalised per column and missing values are imputed to the median. Two channels score 1.0 when they behave alike — originate vs amplify, fast vs slow, narrow vs wide — regardless of their position in the citation network. Complements the structural equivalence matrix, which asks the opposite question.
